// The problem

Social media is broken

Every major platform made the same trade. Your attention — and your data — in exchange for a free service. You were never the customer. You were the product.

It wasn't always this bad. But somewhere along the way, "connecting people" became "maximizing engagement at any cost." Outrage drives clicks. Clicks drive ad revenue. And you're caught in the middle.

Zine starts from the opposite assumption: that a social network can be useful, honest, and sustainable without mining your personal life to pay for it.

👁️
You're the product
Every scroll, pause, and like is tracked, profiled, and sold to advertisers without your meaningful consent.
🧠
Algorithms hijack your attention
Your feed is engineered to keep you outraged, anxious, and online longer — not to show you what you actually care about.
🔒
Your account can vanish overnight
One policy change, one automated false positive, and years of content and connections disappear. You own nothing.
🌐
Walled gardens trap you
Your followers on one platform can't reach you on another. Every network is an island, by design, to keep you locked in.

// How it works

A network with no center

Zine runs on a federated protocol. Instead of one company owning all the servers, anyone can run a Zine node — and all nodes talk to each other seamlessly.

01
Pick a node or run your own
Join any existing Zine node, or spin up your own server in minutes. You're never forced onto a node you don't trust.
02
Your identity travels with you
Your handle and followers aren't locked to one server. Move nodes anytime without losing your account or your audience.
03
Follow anyone, anywhere
A user on one Zine node can follow and interact with a user on any other. The network is open by default, not by exception.
04
Your data, your export
Every piece of content you post is yours to export, move, or delete at any time. No holding your own posts hostage.

// Features

Built different, by design

Zine isn't a Twitter clone with a new coat of paint. It's a rethink of what a social network's responsibilities actually are.

📰
Chronological feed
You see posts in the order they were made. No ranking, no boosting, no mystery as to why something appears. What you follow is what you get.
No algorithm
🔐
Encrypted direct messages
Private conversations are end-to-end encrypted. Not even the node operator can read your DMs. Actual privacy, not a privacy policy.
E2E encryption
🪪
Portable identity
Your account is yours, not the server's. Move between nodes without starting over. Your followers come with you.
Self-sovereign
🚫
No ads, ever
There is no advertising model in Zine because there's no single company to sell ads. Nodes can be community-funded, self-hosted, or subscription-supported.
Ad-free

Open protocol

Open standard
🛡️

// Philosophy

Why we're building this

Social media doesn't have to be extractive. The current model — free service in exchange for surveillance — was a choice, not an inevitability.

Federated social networks have existed for years. The technology works. What's been missing is something that feels as polished and easy to use as the centralized alternatives. That's the gap Zine is trying to close.

We're not building a startup with an exit strategy. Zine is a community project. The protocol is open, the code is public, and no single entity — including us — can take it away from you.

🌍
No single point of failure
If any one node goes down, the rest of the network keeps running. No single server holds it all together.
📖
Radical transparency
The code is public. The protocol is documented. Anyone can audit, fork, or build on top of what we're making.
⚖️
Community governance
Protocol changes go through open discussion and consensus — not a boardroom or a single founder's decision.
♾️
Built to last
No VC pressure, no growth-at-all-costs mandate. Zine is built to be around in 20 years, not flipped in 5.
